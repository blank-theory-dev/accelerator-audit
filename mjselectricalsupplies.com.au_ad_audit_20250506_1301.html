Gemini Vision UX Analysis Summary

Overall Summary:

The mjselectricalsupplies.com.au product page presents a clean and professional appearance, effectively showcasing product information and key trust signals like "Australian Company" and "Easy Returns". Strong points include clear product imagery, pricing, and readily available quantity adjustment. However, the most critical weakness is the lack of a sticky "Add to Cart" button, significantly impacting user experience and potentially conversion rates on both desktop and mobile. A primary opportunity for improvement lies in optimizing the mobile layout to reduce the excessive scrolling required to access all product information and key CTAs. The persistent "Someone in Fairfield East bought..." notification, while potentially positive, is intrusive and should be redesigned.

--- Product Page Findings: ---

Issue/Opportunity: Absence of a Sticky "Add to Cart" Button

Impact: Users must scroll back to the top of the page to add the product to their cart after reviewing specifications, details, or trust signals, creating unnecessary friction and potentially reducing conversions. This is consistent across both desktop and mobile.

Recommendation: Implement a sticky "Add to Cart" button that remains visible at the bottom of the screen throughout the entire page scroll on both desktop and mobile.

Issue/Opportunity: Inconsistent placement of Key Information (Desktop vs. Mobile)

Impact: On desktop, crucial information like trust signals and payment options is above the fold, facilitating a quick purchase decision. On mobile, this same information is fragmented and requires substantial scrolling, potentially causing users to abandon the purchase process. The "Someone in Fairfield..." banner appears at different locations within the mobile scroll.

Recommendation: Maintain consistent placement of crucial product information (price, quantity, "Add to Cart," payment options, trust signals) above the fold on mobile. Prioritize this information before secondary content like customer testimonials or product details. Implement a consistent positioning for the "Someone bought..." banner across mobile screens to avoid user confusion.

Issue/Opportunity: Intrusive "Someone Bought..." Notification

Impact: The constantly present "Someone in Fairfield East bought..." notification, while potentially leveraging social proof, is distracting and covers up important content and design elements across various scroll positions on mobile screens. The large size and frequent appearance is problematic.

Recommendation: Reduce the size and/or change the notification style to a less obtrusive design. Consider using a smaller, less prominent banner, or possibly only showing it once per visit, or based on other triggers than simple scrolling. A/B testing variations would be beneficial.

Issue/Opportunity: Mobile Navigation and Layout Inefficiencies

Impact: The mobile layout requires significant scrolling to see all product details, specifications, and other key information. This cumbersome experience may lead to users abandoning the purchase process. The excessive use of expandable sections also makes information discovery inefficient.

Recommendation: Refine the mobile layout to condense essential information and display it more efficiently within the viewport. Consider reducing the vertical real estate required for various sections, and possibly making some information more easily accessible instead of using collapsible menus.

Issue/Opportunity: Lack of Clear Visual Hierarchy on Mobile

Impact: On mobile, the visual hierarchy isn't consistently clear, making it challenging for users to quickly identify crucial information such as pricing, quantity selectors, and the "Add to Cart" button.

Recommendation: Improve visual hierarchy on mobile through the use of clear typography, spacing, color, and contrast. Highlight key actions with more pronounced visual cues.

Issue/Opportunity: Footer placement and visibility (Mobile)

Impact: The footer content (contact info, about us, etc.) on mobile is positioned very low and requires extensive scrolling to be viewed. Users may miss critical information.

Recommendation: Evaluate if the footer elements are essential. If so, consider alternative placement for easy access on mobile, or consolidate the footer to a more accessible position.

--- Key Recommendations Summary: ---

1. Implement a sticky "Add to Cart" button on both desktop and mobile.

2. Optimize the mobile layout for better information hierarchy and reduce unnecessary scrolling. Consolidate key purchase information.

3. Redesign the "Someone Bought..." notification to be less intrusive and more visually appealing. A/B test variations.
